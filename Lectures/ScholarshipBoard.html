Top Fully-Funded Scholarships

1. Erasmus Mundus Joint Masters (EMJM)

FULLY FUNDED

European Commission's Flagship Scholarship

Benefits:

  • • €1,400/month living allowance (€33,600 total)
  • • Full tuition fee coverage
  • • Travel & installation costs
  • • Health insurance
  • • Study in 2-3 European countries
  • • Joint/Multiple degree from top universities

Key Dates (Fall 2026):

  • • Opens: October 2025
  • • Deadline: December 2025 - January 2026
  • • Results: April 2026
  • • Can apply to max 3 programs

Fields Available:

CS, AI, Data Science, Engineering, Robotics, Environmental, Business, Health & more (150+ programs)

Official: erasmus-plus.ec.europa.eu

Tip: Search the EMJM catalogue for programs in your field. Each program has its own portal.

2. DAAD Scholarships (Germany)

FULLY FUNDED

German Academic Exchange Service - 86+ Programs

Benefits:

  • • €992/month for Masters (€1,300 for PhD)
  • • Travel allowance
  • • Health, accident & liability insurance
  • • Study allowance (€460/year)
  • • German language course (if needed)

Key Programs for Indians:

  • • EPOS (Development-related) - 2 yrs exp needed
  • • Study Scholarships - All Disciplines
  • • Helmut-Schmidt (Public Policy)
  • • STEM Scholarships

Key Dates:

  • • Winter Semester: July 15, 2025
  • • Summer Semester: January 15, 2026
  • • EPOS: October 31, 2025
  • • Start preparing: August 2025

Requirements:

  • • Bachelor's ≤6 years old
  • • English/German proficiency
  • • Some need 2 years work exp

Official: daad.de | DAAD India

Success Rate: ~10% for Indian students. Strong academic profile essential!

3. Swedish Institute (SISGP) - Sweden

FULLY FUNDED

~300 scholarships for Global Professionals

Benefits:

  • • Full tuition coverage
  • • SEK 12,000/month (~€1,050) living
  • • Travel grant SEK 15,000 (~€1,300)
  • • Health insurance
  • • Network membership (NFGL)

Timeline for Fall 2026:

  • • Apply to universities: Oct 16, 2025 - Jan 15, 2026
  • • SI Scholarship portal: Feb 9-25, 2026
  • • Results: March 26, 2026

Requirements:

  • • 3,000+ hours work experience
  • • Leadership experience required
  • • India is eligible country ✓

Official: si.se

Note: India NOT in eligible countries list for SISGP 2026! Check annually.

4. Eiffel Excellence Scholarship (France)

PARTIAL

French Ministry for Europe & Foreign Affairs

Benefits:

  • • €1,200/month allowance (Masters)
  • • International transport
  • • Health insurance
  • • Housing assistance
  • • Cultural activities
  • • Tuition NOT covered (but often waived for public unis)

Key Dates:

  • • Opens: October 1, 2025
  • • Deadline: January 8, 2026
  • • Results: March 30, 2026

Important:

  • • Age limit: ≤29 years for Masters
  • • Apply THROUGH French university
  • • Priority fields: Engineering, Science, Law, Economics

Official: campusfrance.org

5. NL Scholarship & University Scholarships (Netherlands)

PARTIAL + FULL

NL Scholarship:

  • • €5,000 (one-time)
  • • For non-EEA students
  • • Opens: November 2025
  • • Deadline: Feb 1, 2026

University-Specific (Full):

  • • Maastricht NL-High Potential: €36,000 (21 scholarships)
  • • TU Delft: Various excellence scholarships
  • • Utrecht Excellence: Full tuition + stipend
  • • Leiden LExS: €10,000-€15,000

Key Dates:

  • • Applications open: November 1, 2025
  • • Program application: Dec 1-8, 2025
  • • Scholarship deadline: Feb 1, 2026

Requirements:

  • • GPA ≥7.5/10 (or 75%)
  • • Age ≤35 for some
  • • Not previously studied in NL

Official: studyinnl.org

Preparation Checklist for 6th Sem Students

Do NOW (Before Dec 2025):

  • Take IELTS/TOEFL (score needed by Dec)
  • Take GRE (if required by program)
  • Get transcripts & degree certificate
  • Request 2-3 recommendation letters
  • Draft SOP & motivation letters
  • Research specific programs

Documents Required:

  • Passport (valid 2+ years)
  • Bachelor's degree/provisional certificate
  • All semester marksheets
  • English proficiency certificate
  • CV/Resume (use templates provided)
  • Work experience certificates (if any)
  • Research papers/publications (if any)

Smart Application Strategy

1

Apply to Multiple Scholarships

3 Erasmus Mundus + 2-3 DAAD + Country-specific = 6-8 applications

2

Mix of Countries

Germany (DAAD) + Netherlands + France + 1-2 more for backup

3

Strong Profile Building

Good GPA (>8/10), internships, projects, publications help immensely

4

Start Early

Best students start preparing 12-18 months before intake with Reserach Papers, Projects
